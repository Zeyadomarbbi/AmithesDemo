View results in the right-hand panel

The four steps we had modeled in the waterfall.
Two key indicators: TVPI and IRR (rate of return: 8%).
Below this rate, the waterfall changes—the break-even hurdle → the target multiple that allows us to reach 8% and repay the LPs.

Yann will present this table in a committee and modify the values live.
Example: “If we sell today at 10, it’s more attractive than selling at 12 in three years; the hurdle model tells us we should sell today.”

Projection of capital calls
Yann wants to project cash flow with our funds:
“I know I will call up to X; I make four capital calls, one per quarter, where I call a quarter of my management fee.”

The idea is to understand how often the client makes capital calls—quarterly, monthly, or all at the beginning of the year—and whether they call all their management fees at once or spread them quarterly.

⚠️ The treasury plan must be aligned with my business plan (BP).

Bridge
Here, Yann wants to model a “bridge” on realized cash flows to see the impact (e.g., would we have made more money by borrowing from the bank?).

He enters the interest rate, structuring fees, setup costs, etc., and then starts the simulation.
